OPERATIVE REPORT

Patient Name: Olivia Martin
MRN: 452198765
Date of Service: October 22, 2025
Surgeon: Dr. Aiden Patel, MD

PROCEDURES PERFORMED:
1. Arthroscopic rotator cuff repair, left shoulder
2. Arthroscopic labral repair, left shoulder

INDICATIONS:
45-year-old female with progressive shoulder pain and weakness. Clinical exam and imaging suggested combined rotator cuff and superior labral pathology after a workplace fall.

PREOPERATIVE DIAGNOSIS:
1. Symptomatic rotator cuff tear, left shoulder
2. Superior labral tear, left shoulder

POSTOPERATIVE DIAGNOSIS:
Same as above.

PROCEDURE IN DETAIL:
The patient was brought to the operating room and positioned in modified beach chair. After routine preparation and draping, standard posterior viewing portal was established with an anterior working portal.

DIAGNOSTIC ARTHROSCOPY:
Global inspection revealed fraying of the superior labrum with instability at the 11 to 1 o'clock position. A full-thickness tear involving the supraspinatus was visualized. Biceps anchor appeared intact.

LABRAL REPAIR:
Unstable labral tissue was lightly debrided. Knotless anchors were used to reapproximate the labrum to the glenoid rim with stable fixation.

ROTATOR CUFF REPAIR:
Attention was directed to the supraspinatus tear. The footprint was cleared of soft tissue and prepared for repair. Two medial row anchors were placed with lateral row fixation to restore footprint coverage.

POSTOPERATIVE PLAN:
Arm immobilizer applied in the operating room. Outpatient pain regimen and sling instructions provided. Physical therapy protocol to be determined at first postoperative visit.

ESTIMATED BLOOD LOSS: Not documented.
COMPLICATIONS: None.
DRAINS: None.

IMPLANTS:
Two knotless labral anchors and four rotator cuff anchors placed.

DISPOSITION:
Transferred to PACU in stable condition.
