## Required Pre-operative Documentation Elements (Per Guidelines)

According to the labral repair clinical guidelines, the following pre-operative documentation must be present:

### 1. Function-Limiting Pain
- **Requirement:** Documentation of pain that significantly limits functional activities
- **Current Status:** ❌ **MISSING** - The operative report mentions "increasing pain" but lacks specific details about functional limitations

### 2. Failed Conservative Management
- **Requirement:** Documentation of failure of provider-directed non-surgical management for **at least three (3) months** in duration
- **Current Status:** ❌ **MISSING** - No documentation of:
  - Physical therapy attempts
  - Medication trials
  - Activity modifications
  - Injection therapies
  - Duration of conservative treatment

### 3. Advanced Diagnostic Imaging
- **Requirement:** MRI or CT scan that:
  - Demonstrates the labral tear (SLAP, Bankart, or other types)
  - Correlates with reported symptoms
  - Correlates with physical examination findings
- **Current Status:** ⚠️ **PARTIALLY DOCUMENTED** - The operative report states:
  - "Preoperative evaluation revealed a posteroinferior labrum tear"
  - However, **no actual imaging report** or specific imaging findings are included in this chart
  - Missing: Imaging study date, type, detailed findings, and correlation with clinical presentation

### 4. Exclusion of Other Pathological Conditions
- **Requirement:** Documentation that the following conditions have been excluded:
  - Fracture
  - Thoracic outlet syndrome
  - Brachial plexus disorders
  - Referred neck pain
  - Cervical radiculopathy
  - Advanced glenohumeral osteoarthritis
- **Current Status:** ❌ **MISSING** - No documentation of differential diagnosis workup or exclusion of these conditions

### 5. Range of Motion Assessment
- **Requirement:** Documentation showing "minimally limited or full shoulder range of motion"
- **Current Status:** ❌ **MISSING** - No pre-operative range of motion measurements documented
  - The operative report only mentions examination under anesthesia during surgery
  - No baseline functional assessment available

### 6. Positive Orthopedic Tests
- **Requirement:** Documentation of **ONE or MORE** positive results from:
  - O'Brien's Test
  - Biceps Load Test
  - Clunk Test
  - Anterior Slide Test
  - Compression Rotation Test
  - Speed's Test
  - Modified dynamic labral shear test
- **Current Status:** ❌ **MISSING** - No documentation of any pre-operative orthopedic special tests performed

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions Required

1. **Obtain Pre-operative Medical Records**
   - Request office visit notes from the surgeon's clinic
   - Obtain physical therapy records (if applicable)
   - Collect imaging reports (MRI/CT scans)

2. **Verify Documentation Completeness**
   - Confirm 3+ months of conservative management is documented elsewhere
   - Verify orthopedic tests were performed (even if results were negative)
   - Confirm imaging studies correlate with clinical findings

3. **Documentation Best Practices for Future Cases**
   - Ensure pre-operative clinic notes include all guideline requirements
   - Document conservative management attempts with specific dates
   - Include imaging reports in the medical record
   - Document all orthopedic tests performed (positive and negative results)
   - Create a checklist based on guideline requirements

### Documentation That Should Have Been Included

A complete pre-operative chart for labral repair should contain:
1. **Initial evaluation note** with detailed history and physical examination
2. **Functional assessment** documenting pain impact on daily activities
3. **Conservative management notes** spanning at least 3 months
4. **Imaging reports** (MRI/CT) with radiologist interpretation
5. **Physical examination documentation** including:
   - Range of motion measurements
   - Orthopedic special test results
   - Neurological assessment
6. **Differential diagnosis** documentation showing exclusion of other conditions
7. **Treatment plan** documenting progression from conservative to surgical management
